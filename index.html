Welcome to the Durga Puja Guide

Durga Puja is not just a festival; it's an emotion deeply embedded in the heart of every Bengali. Celebrated with grandeur and enthusiasm, it marks the victory of good over evil as Goddess Durga triumphs over Mahishasura. Kolkata is renowned for its intricate pandals and vibrant celebrations. Each year, the city transforms into a cultural hub where art, religion, and community come together. With this guide, immerse yourself in the grandeur of Durga Puja and explore the rich cultural heritage.

Why is Durga Puja Special?

Durga Puja transcends religious boundaries and has evolved into a social-cultural event. The pandals showcase remarkable artistry, from traditional to modern themes, making them popular attractions. From North Kolkataâ€™s heritage-style pujas to the artistic innovations of South Kolkata, every nook of the city comes alive during this festive season. Popular pujas include the ones at Santosh Mitra Square, Suruchi Sangha, and Tala Prattoy. Each offers a unique display of devotion combined with art and creativity.

Apart from Kolkata, towns like Siliguri, Bardhaman, and Howrah also host grand pujas. The beating of dhak (drums), the aroma of dhuno (incense), and the delicious bhog (offerings) define the spirit of the festival.
